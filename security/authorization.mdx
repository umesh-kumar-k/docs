Authorization techniques control **what authenticated principals can access/do**: **RBAC** (roles), **ABAC** (attributes), **ReBAC** (relationships), **PBAC** (policies), **DAC** (owner discretion), **MAC** (central labels)—policy engines (OPA/Cerbos) + data stores (Zanzibar/SpiceDB) externalize decisions from apps for scale/flexibility.[cerbos] Senior architects choose RBAC for simplicity (org hierarchies), ABAC/ReBAC for fine-grained (SaaS/multi-tenant), PBAC for dynamic (runtime context), trading policy complexity for centralized governance.[1]

## Interview Summary
**RBAC**: User → Role → Permission (static); **ABAC**: User attrs + Resource attrs + Env → Policy eval (dynamic); **ReBAC**: Graph relationships (user owns project → edit docs); **PBAC**: YAML/Rego policies + PDP (OPA); **DAC**: Owner grants (file shares); **MAC**: Labels/clearance (military). **PDP**: Policy Decision Point (OPA/Cerbos), **PEP**: Policy Enforcement Point (app/gateway).[cerbos][2]

## Keywords & Patterns
- **RBAC**: Role hierarchies, Perm-Role/User-Role matrices.[cerbos]
- **ABAC**: XACML-like (subject/object/env attrs), PDP eval.[1]
- **ReBAC**: Zanzibar tuples (user:doc:viewer), graph traversal.[3]
- **PBAC**: Rego/YAML policies, external PDP (OPA).[4]
- **DAC/MAC**: Owner-controlled vs system-enforced.[cerbos]
- **Externalized**: App → PDP query → allow/deny + obligations.[1]

## Common Trade-offs + Questions
RBAC: Simple/static vs inflexible; ABAC: Granular/dynamic vs policy explosion; ReBAC: Natural hierarchies vs graph complexity; PBAC: Centralized vs latency/OPA learning curve; DAC: Flexible vs insecure; MAC: Secure vs rigid.[cerbos][2]

**Example questions**:
- SaaS multi-tenant? (ABAC: tenant_id attr).[1]
- Social permissions? (ReBAC: friends/followers).[3]
- Dynamic policies? (PBAC: OPA Rego).[cerbos]

| Model | Granularity | Complexity | Scale | Flexibility |
|-------|-------------|------------|-------|-------------|
| RBAC | Role-level | Low | High | Static [cerbos] |
| ABAC | Attr-level | High | Medium | Dynamic [1] |
| ReBAC | Relationship | High | High | Natural [3] |
| PBAC | Policy-level | Medium | High | Programmable [4] |

## Use Cases
- **RBAC**: Enterprise HR (employee/manager roles).[cerbos]
- **ABAC**: SaaS (tenant+role+time).[1]
- **ReBAC**: Social (friends/documents).[3]
- **PBAC**: Microservices (OPA sidecar).[cerbos]
- **MAC**: Military/gov classified.[cerbos]

## Big Tech Blogs & Efficiency Wins
**Google Zanzibar**: ReBAC powers YouTube/Drive/Calendar (2B users, 99.999% availability). **Netflix**: ABAC + Casbin reduced permission bugs 90%. **Uber**: OPA Rego centralized 100+ service auth (policy-as-code).[5][6][7]

## Cheat-Sheet Q&A
**Q: RBAC vs ABAC?** A: Roles (static) vs attrs (dynamic).[cerbos]  
**Q: Zanzibar tuples?** A: `user:doc#viewer@group:team#member`.[3]
**Q: OPA Rego example?** A: `allow if input.user.role == "admin"`.[4]
**Q: PDP vs PEP?** A: Decision (PDP) vs Enforcement (PEP).[cerbos]  
**Q: ReBAC traversal?** A: Graph BFS/DFS (permission inheritance).[3]

## Tools/Frameworks
- **RBAC**: Keycloak, Auth0 Roles.[cerbos]
- **ABAC**: OPA Rego, XACML (legacy).[1]
- **ReBAC**: SpiceDB, Authzed, Permify (Zanzibar).[3]
- **PBAC**: Cerbos (YAML), OPA (Rego).[cerbos]
- **Java**: Spring Security `@PreAuthorize`, Casbin-Java.[8]

## Data Structures & Algorithms
**RBAC**: Role-Perm/User-Role matrices (adjacency lists). **ABAC**: Policy trees (AND/OR eval). **ReBAC**: Graph traversal (BFS/DFS tuples), materialized views. **Caching**: LRU PDP responses (principal+resource).[cerbos][3]

## Key Highlights & Tradeoffs
**RBAC**: Start simple, scales poorly  
**ABAC**: Flexible, policy explosion  
**ReBAC**: Natural hierarchies, graph ops  
**PBAC**: Centralized, latency/vendor  

**Interviews**: "RBAC early, ReBAC social, PBAC+OPA enterprise"
