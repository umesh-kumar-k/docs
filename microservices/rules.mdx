Microservices commandments enforce loose coupling, autonomy, and resilience through principles like single responsibility, no shared persistence, and self-discovery to avoid distributed monoliths.[1][2]

## Key Patterns
Stateless services with dedicated DBs per service; lightweight async messaging (gRPC/Kafka) over shared libs; self-registration via Consul/Eureka; explicit policy checks at boundaries; polyglot stacks with independent CI/CD pipelines.  IDEALS framework: Interface segregation, Deployability, Event-driven, Availability>Consistency, Loose-coupling, Single responsibility.[2][3][1]

## Use Cases & Tools
E-commerce isolates payments/inventory DBs for independent scaling; Netflix applies commandments post-2008 outage for 700+ resilient services.  Tools: Docker/Kubernetes for isolation, Consul for discovery, Kafka for events, Istio for traffic/policy enforcement, ArgoCD for GitOps deploys.[4][5][6][2]

Big tech refs: Netflix TechBlog on commandments enabling chaos-safe deploys (netflixtechblog.com); Uber Eng on polyglot + no-affinity cutting scaling pains (eng.uber.com).[5][6]
